Exercises: Modality

Practice your understanding of modality with these exercises. Test your knowledge of modal verbs, semi-modals, and modal expressions for different functions such as obligation, possibility, probability, and permission.

Back to Exercises
Review Modality Grammar

Exercise 1: Modal Verbs for Different Functions

Choose the most appropriate modal verb to complete each sentence based on the function indicated in brackets.

1. You __________________ wear a seatbelt when driving. It's the law. (obligation)

must

2. It's getting cloudy. It __________________ rain later today. (possibility)

might/may/could

3. You __________________ be tired after such a long journey. Why don't you rest? (deduction)

must

4. __________________ I use your phone? Mine is out of battery. (permission)

Could/May/Can

5. You __________________ smoke in this building. It's prohibited. (prohibition)

must not/cannot/may not

6. We __________________ leave now if we want to catch the last train. (necessity)

have to/need to/must

7. You __________________ worry about it. I'll take care of everything. (lack of necessity)

don't have to/needn't

8. She __________________ speak three languages fluently. It's impressive! (ability)

can

Exercise 2: Past Modals

Complete each sentence with the appropriate past modal form.

1. You __________________ me about the change in plans. I was waiting for hours! (obligation in the past)

should have told

2. She __________________ to the party, but she decided to stay home instead. (possibility that didn't happen)

could have gone

3. He __________________ the email. I sent it yesterday. (deduction about the past)

must have received

4. They __________________ the directions. That's why they got lost. (negative deduction about the past)

can't/couldn't have followed

5. We __________________ a taxi, but we decided to walk instead. (lack of necessity in the past)

didn't have to take/didn't need to take/needn't have taken

Exercise 3: Modal Expressions

Rewrite each sentence using the modal expression given in brackets.

1. I'm sure she is at home now. (be bound to)

She is bound to be at home now.

2. It's possible that he will arrive late. (be likely to)

He is likely to arrive late.

3. I'm certain they didn't notice the mistake. (can't have)

They can't have noticed the mistake.

4. Perhaps we should consider another option. (might as well)

We might as well consider another option.

5. It's not necessary for you to attend the meeting. (don't have to)

You don't have to attend the meeting.

Exercise 4: Degrees of Certainty

Choose the modal verb that expresses the appropriate degree of certainty for each situation.

1. Look at those dark clouds. It __________________ rain soon. (strong probability)

must

2. I'm not sure where John is. He __________________ be at the library. (possibility)

might

3. That __________________ be true! I don't believe it. (impossibility)

can't

4. She's been studying all year. She __________________ pass the exam. (logical conclusion)

should

5. The flight __________________ be delayed due to the storm. (strong possibility)

is likely to

Exercise 5: Error Correction

Each sentence below contains an error in the use of modals. Identify and correct the error.

1. You must to arrive by 9 AM tomorrow.

You must arrive by 9 AM tomorrow. (Remove "to" after "must")

2. She can't to swim very well.

She can't swim very well. (Remove "to" after "can't")

3. I must can finish this report by tomorrow.

I must finish this report by tomorrow. (Use only one modal verb)

4. They should to have told us about the change in plans.

They should have told us about the change in plans. (Remove "to" after "should")

5. You don't must go to the meeting.

You don't have to go to the meeting. OR You needn't go to the meeting. (Use "don't have to" or "needn't" instead of "don't must")
