1. Property and Home

Key Vocabulary

  • Property development
  • Land use options
  • Real estate terminology
  • Housing features
  • Location considerations

Useful Phrases

  • "To be honest, I wouldn't recommend..." (for giving advice)
  • "To be frank..." (for expressing honest opinions)
  • "Absolutely not..." (for strong disagreement)
  • "It would be great for..." (for highlighting advantages)
  • "The thing is there are advantages and disadvantages to both plans."
  • "The downside is that..." (for discussing disadvantages)
  • "We can only afford..." (for discussing financial limitations)
  • "We can get a house if..." (for discussing conditional possibilities)

Discussion Points

  • Comparing city living versus suburban/rural living
  • Discussing property development options
  • Weighing advantages and disadvantages of different housing choices
  • Expressing preferences about living arrangements
  • Discussing investment value of different property types

2. Experiences and Opinions

Key Vocabulary

  • Holiday experiences
  • Leisure activities
  • Entertainment preferences
  • Literary criticism
  • Television programming

Useful Phrases

  • "What about ordinary people?" (for expressing concerns about fairness)
  • "Can't we have some peace and quiet, too?" (for expressing desires)
  • "That's what we tell ourselves." (for challenging assumptions)
  • "We're fooling ourselves." (for expressing skepticism)
  • "I thought it was badly written." (for expressing negative opinions)
  • "I can't agree." (for polite disagreement)
  • "I thought it was absolutely hilarious." (for strong positive opinions)
  • "But it was utterly predictable." (for criticism)

Discussion Points

  • Describing positive and negative travel experiences
  • Expressing and justifying opinions about books and TV shows
  • Discussing different perspectives on entertainment
  • Comparing viewing habits and preferences
  • Debating the quality and value of different forms of entertainment

3. Motivation for Language Learning

Key Vocabulary

  • Language acquisition
  • Learning strategies
  • Motivational factors
  • Professional requirements
  • Cultural appreciation

Useful Phrases

  • "I started learning... so I could..." (for explaining motivation)
  • "If I wanted to..., I had to learn." (for explaining necessity)
  • "Because work mainly." (for explaining professional motivation)
  • "I fell in love with..." (for expressing passion for learning)
  • "Everything is done in English." (for explaining language dominance)

Discussion Points

  • Discussing various motivations for language learning
  • Comparing different language learning approaches
  • Debating fluency versus accuracy in language learning
  • Describing strategies for vocabulary retention
  • Discussing experiences of language immersion
