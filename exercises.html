Tenses Exercises

Exercise 1: Complete the sentences with the correct tense

  1. I ________ (study) English for five years now.
  2. She ________ (work) at the company when she met her husband.
  3. By the time we arrived, the movie ________ (already/start).
  4. I ________ (go) to the gym three times a week.
  5. This time tomorrow, I ________ (take) my final exam.
  1. I have been studying English for five years now.
  2. She was working at the company when she met her husband.
  3. By the time we arrived, the movie had already started.
  4. I go to the gym three times a week.
  5. This time tomorrow, I will be taking my final exam.

Exercise 2: Identify and correct the errors in these sentences

  1. I am living in London since 2010.
  2. She has went to the store yesterday.
  3. We are having a car for ten years.
  4. By next month, I will finished my course.
  5. I am knowing the answer to that question.
  1. I have been living in London since 2010.
  2. She went to the store yesterday.
  3. We have had a car for ten years.
  4. By next month, I will have finished my course.
  5. I know the answer to that question.

Reporting Exercises

Exercise 1: Convert the following direct speech to reported speech

  1. "I am studying for my exam," said John.
  2. "Where did you go yesterday?" asked Mary.
  3. "Don't touch that!" the teacher warned.
  4. "I will call you tomorrow," she promised.
  5. "I have never been to Paris," Tom admitted.
  1. John said (that) he was studying for his exam.
  2. Mary asked where I/he/she had gone the day before.
  3. The teacher warned not to touch that.
  4. She promised to call me/him/her the next day.
  5. Tom admitted (that) he had never been to Paris.

Exercise 2: Convert the following reported speech to direct speech

  1. She said she was feeling unwell.
  2. He asked me if I had seen his keys.
  3. The doctor advised me to rest for a few days.
  4. They complained that the service was terrible.
  5. My friend suggested going to the cinema.
  1. "I am feeling unwell," she said.
  2. "Have you seen my keys?" he asked me.
  3. "You should rest for a few days," the doctor advised me.
  4. "The service is terrible," they complained.
  5. "Let's go to the cinema," my friend suggested.

Narrative Tenses Exercises

Exercise 1: Complete the story with the correct narrative tenses

It was (be) a cold winter evening. I ______ (walk) home from work when suddenly I ______ (hear) a strange noise. I ______ (stop) to listen. The noise ______ (come) from a small alley. I ______ (never/be) down this alley before, but curiosity ______ (get) the better of me. As I ______ (approach) the alley, I ______ (realize) that someone ______ (follow) me for the last few minutes. I ______ (turn) around quickly, but there ______ (be) no one there.

It was (be) a cold winter evening. I was walking (walk) home from work when suddenly I heard (hear) a strange noise. I stopped (stop) to listen. The noise was coming (come) from a small alley. I had never been (never/be) down this alley before, but curiosity got (get) the better of me. As I approached (approach) the alley, I realized (realize) that someone had been following (follow) me for the last few minutes. I turned (turn) around quickly, but there was (be) no one there.

Exercise 2: Rewrite the sentences using the correct narrative tense

  1. She arrives at the party. She sees her ex-boyfriend. (past simple)
  2. I eat dinner. The phone rings. (past continuous and past simple)
  3. He doesn't recognize her. They meet five years ago. (past simple and past perfect)
  4. We wait for an hour. The bus finally comes. (past perfect continuous and past simple)
  5. I read a book. I fall asleep. (past continuous and past simple)
  1. She arrived at the party. She saw her ex-boyfriend.
  2. I was eating dinner when the phone rang.
  3. He didn't recognize her. They had met five years ago.
  4. We had been waiting for an hour when the bus finally came.
  5. I was reading a book when I fell asleep.
