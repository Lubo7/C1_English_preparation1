Exercises: Passive Structures

Practice your understanding of passive structures with these exercises. Test your knowledge of passive forms in different tenses, passive reporting structures, and passive infinitives and gerunds.

Back to Exercises
Review Passive Structures Grammar

Exercise 1: Active to Passive Transformation

Rewrite each active sentence in the passive voice, keeping the same meaning.

1. The company employs over 500 people.

Over 500 people are employed by the company.

2. They are building a new shopping center in the city.

A new shopping center is being built in the city.

3. Someone has stolen my wallet.

My wallet has been stolen.

4. The government will announce the new policy tomorrow.

The new policy will be announced by the government tomorrow.

5. They had completed the project before the deadline.

The project had been completed before the deadline.

Exercise 2: Passive with Different Tenses

Complete each sentence with the correct passive form of the verb in brackets, using the tense indicated.

1. The museum __________________ (visit) by thousands of tourists every year. (present simple)

is visited

2. The new bridge __________________ (construct) at the moment. (present continuous)

is being constructed

3. The report __________________ (submit) to the committee last week. (past simple)

was submitted

4. By the time we arrived, the decision __________________ (make) already. (past perfect)

had been made

5. The new system __________________ (implement) by next month. (future perfect)

will have been implemented

6. The documents __________________ (sign) by all parties. (present perfect)

have been signed

7. The building __________________ (renovate) when the fire broke out. (past continuous)

was being renovated

8. The conference __________________ (organize) next summer. (future simple)

will be organized

Exercise 3: Passive Reporting Structures

Rewrite each sentence using passive reporting structures.

1. People say that the company is in financial trouble.

It is said that the company is in financial trouble. OR The company is said to be in financial trouble.

2. They believe that the missing child has been found.

It is believed that the missing child has been found. OR The missing child is believed to have been found.

3. People expect that the government will reduce taxes next year.

It is expected that the government will reduce taxes next year. OR The government is expected to reduce taxes next year.

4. They reported that the president had arrived safely.

It was reported that the president had arrived safely. OR The president was reported to have arrived safely.

5. People think that the suspect is hiding abroad.

It is thought that the suspect is hiding abroad. OR The suspect is thought to be hiding abroad.

Exercise 4: Passive Infinitives and Gerunds

Complete each sentence with the correct passive infinitive or gerund form of the verb in brackets.

1. The criminal wants __________________ (release) from prison.

to be released

2. She enjoys __________________ (invite) to important events.

being invited

3. The manager expects the report __________________ (complete) by tomorrow.

to be completed

4. They avoided __________________ (see) by the security guards.

being seen

5. The painting deserves __________________ (display) in a museum.

to be displayed

Exercise 5: Mixed Passive Exercises

Choose the correct passive form to complete each sentence.

1. The new policy __________________ next week.

will be announced

2. The suspect __________________ by the police for three hours.

has been questioned

3. By the time we arrived, the problem __________________.

had been solved

4. The president __________________ to have left the country.

is reported

5. The children __________________ to bed when the storm started.

were being put
