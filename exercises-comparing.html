Comparing and Contrasting Exercises

This page contains practice exercises for comparing and contrasting structures in English. Complete these exercises to reinforce your understanding of comparative and superlative forms, advanced comparative structures, and parallel structures.

Exercise 1: Comparative and Superlative Forms

Complete the sentences with the correct comparative or superlative form of the adjectives in brackets.

  1. This is ________ (good) restaurant in the city.
  2. The exam was ________ (difficult) than we expected.
  3. She speaks English ________ (fluently) than her brother.
  4. This book is ________ (interesting) one I've ever read.
  5. The weather today is ________ (bad) than yesterday.
  6. That was ________ (little) helpful comment you could have made.
  7. The new office building is ________ (tall) than the old one.
  8. This exercise is ________ (challenging) in the whole workbook.
  9. My new apartment is ________ (spacious) than my previous one.
  10. She is ________ (careful) driver in our family.
  1. This is the best restaurant in the city.
  2. The exam was more difficult than we expected.
  3. She speaks English more fluently than her brother.
  4. This book is the most interesting one I've ever read.
  5. The weather today is worse than yesterday.
  6. That was the least helpful comment you could have made.
  7. The new office building is taller than the old one.
  8. This exercise is the most challenging in the whole workbook.
  9. My new apartment is more spacious than my previous one.
  10. She is the most careful driver in our family.

Exercise 2: Advanced Comparative Structures

Complete the sentences using the appropriate comparative structure.

  1. ________ you practice, ________ you will improve. (the more)
  2. This computer is ________ expensive ________ that one. (twice as)
  3. The film was ________ good ________ I had hoped. (not as)
  4. The new version is ________ better than the old one. (considerably)
  5. Is the traffic ________ better today? (any)
  6. The journey took ________ long ________ we expected. (half as)
  7. ________ I learn about this subject, ________ fascinating I find it. (the more)
  8. The results were ________ impressive ________ last year's. (not as)
  9. The new restaurant is ________ better than I thought it would be. (even)
  10. This course is ________ comprehensive ________ the previous one. (three times as)
  1. The more you practice, the more you will improve.
  2. This computer is twice as expensive as that one.
  3. The film was not as good as I had hoped.
  4. The new version is considerably better than the old one.
  5. Is the traffic any better today?
  6. The journey took half as long as we expected.
  7. The more I learn about this subject, the more fascinating I find it.
  8. The results were not as impressive as last year's.
  9. The new restaurant is even better than I thought it would be.
  10. This course is three times as comprehensive as the previous one.

Exercise 3: Parallel Structures

Rewrite the following sentences to create parallel structure.

  1. She enjoys reading books, to watch movies, and playing games.
  2. The advantages of studying abroad include learning a new language, cultural experiences, and you can make international friends.
  3. I would rather walk than taking the bus.
  4. The teacher asked us to read the chapter, that we should take notes, and to prepare questions.
  5. He likes swimming more than to run.
  6. The report discusses the causes of climate change, how to mitigate its effects, and prevention strategies.
  7. She is not only intelligent but also she is hardworking.
  8. The company focuses on developing new products, to improve customer service, and increasing sales.
  9. We can either stay at home or we can go to the restaurant.
  10. The job requires experience in marketing, knowledge of social media, and you must be good at communication.
  1. She enjoys reading books, watching movies, and playing games.
  2. The advantages of studying abroad include learning a new language, experiencing different cultures, and making international friends.
  3. I would rather walk than take the bus.
  4. The teacher asked us to read the chapter, to take notes, and to prepare questions.
  5. He likes swimming more than running.
  6. The report discusses the causes of climate change, the mitigation of its effects, and the prevention strategies.
  7. She is not only intelligent but also hardworking.
  8. The company focuses on developing new products, improving customer service, and increasing sales.
  9. We can either stay at home or go to the restaurant.
  10. The job requires experience in marketing, knowledge of social media, and skill in communication.

Exercise 4: Error Correction

Identify and correct the errors in the following sentences related to comparing and contrasting.

  1. This is the most easiest exercise in the book.
  2. My brother is more taller than me.
  3. She speaks English more better than her sister.
  4. This is the most unique experience I've ever had.
  5. The weather is more colder today than yesterday.
  6. He is the most cleverest student in the class.
  7. This book is more interesting than any book I've read.
  8. She is not as good than her brother at mathematics.
  9. The more you will study, the better results you will get.
  10. I prefer to read books than watching movies.
  1. This is the easiest exercise in the book. (Remove "most" - double superlative)
  2. My brother is taller than me. (Remove "more" - double comparative)
  3. She speaks English better than her sister. (Remove "more" - double comparative)
  4. This is a unique experience I've ever had. (Remove "most" - "unique" is not gradable)
  5. The weather is colder today than yesterday. (Remove "more" - double comparative)
  6. He is the cleverest student in the class. (Remove "most" - double superlative)
  7. This book is more interesting than any other book I've read. (Add "other" for logical comparison)
  8. She is not as good as her brother at mathematics. (Replace "than" with "as")
  9. The more you study, the better results you will get. (Remove "will" from the if-clause)
  10. I prefer reading books to watching movies. (Use parallel structure)
