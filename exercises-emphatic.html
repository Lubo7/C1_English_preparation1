Exercises: Emphatic Structures

Practice your understanding of emphatic structures with these exercises. Test your knowledge of emphatic 'do/does/did', cleft sentences, fronting, inversion, and other emphatic forms.

Back to Exercises
Review Emphatic Structures Grammar

Exercise 1: Emphatic 'Do/Does/Did'

Rewrite each sentence using emphatic 'do/does/did' to add emphasis.

1. I understand your concerns.

I do understand your concerns.

2. She knows the answer.

She does know the answer.

3. They arrived on time, despite the traffic.

They did arrive on time, despite the traffic.

4. I appreciate your efforts.

I do appreciate your efforts.

5. We need your help.

We do need your help.

Exercise 2: It-Cleft Sentences

Rewrite each sentence as an it-cleft sentence, emphasizing the underlined element.

1. John bought a new car yesterday.

It was John who bought a new car yesterday.

2. She needs more experience.

It is more experience that she needs.

3. I met her in Paris.

It was in Paris that I met her.

4. They visited Rome last summer.

It was last summer that they visited Rome.

5. The new marketing strategy increased our sales.

It was the new marketing strategy that increased our sales.

Exercise 3: Wh-Cleft Sentences

Rewrite each sentence as a wh-cleft sentence (pseudo-cleft), emphasizing the underlined element.

1. She needs more experience.

What she needs is more experience.

2. I want a clear explanation.

What I want is a clear explanation.

3. The system crashed.

What happened was the system crashed.

4. We went wrong in our initial planning.

Where we went wrong was in our initial planning.

5. I'm calling to discuss the proposal.

Why I'm calling is to discuss the proposal.

Exercise 4: Fronting and Inversion

Rewrite each sentence using fronting or inversion to create emphasis.

1. I will never forget that day.

That day I will never forget.

2. I have never seen such a beautiful sunset.

Never have I seen such a beautiful sunset.

3. She had hardly finished when the phone rang.

Hardly had she finished when the phone rang.

4. The weather was so bad that we stayed home.

So bad was the weather that we stayed home.

5. If I had known earlier, I would have helped.

Had I known earlier, I would have helped.

Exercise 5: Other Emphatic Structures

Rewrite each sentence using the emphatic structure indicated in brackets.

1. I saw the accident happen. (emphatic reflexive pronoun)

I myself saw the accident happen.

2. I want a cup of tea. (all structure)

All I want is a cup of tea.

3. It was a long time ago. (repetition)

It was a long, long time ago.

4. It's a great film. (emphatic tag)

It's a great film, that one.

5. That was helpful. (intensifier)

That was really/extremely/incredibly helpful.

Exercise 6: Error Correction

Each sentence below contains an error in the use of emphatic structures. Identify and correct the error.

1. It was in Paris where we met.

It was in Paris that we met. (Use "that" not "where" in it-cleft sentences for places)

2. Never I have seen such a beautiful sunset.

Never have I seen such a beautiful sunset. (Invert subject and auxiliary verb)

3. What I want is that you help me.

What I want is for you to help me. (Use "for + object + to-infinitive" not a that-clause)

4. I do can help you.

I can help you. OR I do want to help you. (Don't use emphatic "do" with modal verbs)

5. The president himself's speech was inspiring.

The president's speech itself was inspiring. OR The president himself gave an inspiring speech. (Place emphatic reflexive pronouns after the noun they refer to or after the verb)
