14-Day C1 English Test Preparation Plan

A comprehensive study guide to help you succeed on your C1 English exam

Overview

This comprehensive study plan is designed to prepare you for your C1 English test in 14 days. The plan focuses on both the thematic vocabulary areas from your course materials and the specific grammar topics you need to review. Each day includes a balanced mix of vocabulary, grammar, listening, speaking, reading, and writing practice.

Daily Schedule Structure

Morning Session (1-1.5 hours)

Grammar focus

Afternoon Session (1-1.5 hours)

Vocabulary and thematic content

Evening Session (1 hour)

Practice exercises and review

Day-by-Day Plan

Day 1: Assessment and Review of Tenses

Morning:

  • Complete a diagnostic test
  • Begin review of present tenses

Afternoon:

  • Vocabulary focus: Property and Home
  • Key phrases for describing housing
  • Practice with comparative language

Evening:

  • Grammar exercises on present tenses
  • Vocabulary flashcards
  • Listen to audio excerpts

Day 2: Past Tenses and Experiences

Morning:

  • Review of past tenses
  • Focus on narrative tenses

Afternoon:

  • Vocabulary focus: Experiences and Opinions
  • Language for expressing experiences
  • Phrases for agreeing and disagreeing

Evening:

  • Grammar exercises on past tenses
  • Writing practice with narrative tenses
  • Listen to audio excerpts

Day 3: Future Forms and Language Learning

Morning:

  • Review of future forms
  • Practice with predictions and plans

Afternoon:

  • Vocabulary focus: Motivation for Language Learning
  • Phrases for discussing goals
  • Vocabulary for education

Evening:

  • Grammar exercises on future forms
  • Speaking practice about learning
  • Listen to audio excerpts

Study Resources and Materials

Grammar Resources

  • Create flashcards for each grammar topic
  • Use British Council Learn English resources
  • Practice with exercises from course materials

Vocabulary Resources

  • Create thematic vocabulary lists
  • Use vocabulary in context through practice
  • Review collocations and phrasal verbs

Listening Practice

  • Listen to the audio excerpts from materials
  • Use BBC Radio 4 programs for practice
  • Watch TED Talks related to thematic areas

Speaking Practice

  • Record yourself speaking on topics
  • Practice with a study partner if possible
  • Prepare responses to common questions

Test Day Tips

  1. Get a good night's sleep before the test
  2. Arrive early to reduce stress
  3. Read instructions carefully
  4. Manage your time effectively during the test
  5. Stay calm and focused throughout
  6. Review your answers if time permits

Good luck with your preparation! This plan is designed to be comprehensive but flexible. Adjust the focus areas based on your progress and needs.
