Gender Representation and Equality Vocabulary

This page focuses on vocabulary related to gender representation and equality, a key thematic area in your C1 English course. Understanding this vocabulary will help you discuss gender issues fluently and accurately in your exam.

Back to Vocabulary Themes

Key Vocabulary

Gender Concepts

  • Gender equality - Equal rights, responsibilities and opportunities for all genders
  • Gender equity - Fairness of treatment according to respective needs
  • Gender identity - One's internal sense of their gender
  • Gender bias - Unfair difference in treatment based on gender
  • Gender stereotypes - Oversimplified generalizations about gender attributes
  • Gender-neutral - Not associated with either masculine or feminine qualities
  • Gender gap - Differences between genders, especially in pay or opportunities

Equality and Rights

  • Equal opportunities - Same chances for advancement regardless of gender
  • Equal pay - Same compensation for comparable work regardless of gender
  • Discrimination - Unfair treatment based on gender or other characteristics
  • Empowerment - Process of becoming stronger and more confident
  • Advocacy - Public support for a particular cause or policy
  • Inclusivity - Practice of including people who might otherwise be excluded
  • Diversity - Inclusion of different types of people in a group or organization

Workplace and Professional Context

Workplace Terminology

  • Glass ceiling - Invisible barrier preventing women from rising to top positions
  • Gender pay gap - Difference in average earnings between men and women
  • Maternity/paternity leave - Time off work for parents after childbirth
  • Parental leave - Gender-neutral term for time off for childcare
  • Flexible working - Arrangements allowing employees to vary working hours/location
  • Work-life balance - Equilibrium between career and personal life
  • Mentorship - Guidance provided by an experienced person

Professional Development

  • Career progression - Advancement in one's professional path
  • Leadership roles - Positions involving direction and guidance of others
  • Boardroom diversity - Variety of perspectives in company leadership
  • Quota system - Required minimum number of positions for underrepresented groups
  • Positive discrimination - Favoring underrepresented groups to address imbalances
  • Networking - Interacting with others to exchange information and develop contacts
  • Sponsorship - Active support from senior figures to advance careers

Media and Cultural Representation

Media Representation

  • Gender portrayal - How genders are depicted in media
  • Objectification - Treating a person as an object without regard to dignity
  • Stereotypical roles - Characters that conform to traditional gender expectations
  • Gender-swapping - Changing a character's gender in adaptations
  • Male/female gaze - Perspective that reflects masculine/feminine viewpoints
  • Representation - Portrayal and inclusion of diverse groups
  • Visibility - Extent to which certain groups are seen and recognized

Cultural Analysis

  • Bechdel test - Measure of female representation in fiction
  • Gender roles - Social and behavioral norms considered appropriate for genders
  • Cultural norms - Standards of behavior that are typical in a society
  • Social conditioning - Process of training individuals to behave in a way society approves
  • Implicit bias - Unconscious attitudes affecting understanding and decisions
  • Microaggressions - Subtle, often unintentional expressions of prejudice
  • Intersectionality - Interconnected nature of social categorizations

Useful Phrases for Discussion

Discussing Gender Equality

  • "There's still a significant gender pay gap in many industries."
  • "We need to address the underrepresentation of women in leadership positions."
  • "Gender stereotypes continue to influence career choices."
  • "Equal opportunities should be available regardless of gender."
  • "Policies should be implemented to promote work-life balance for all employees."
  • "Media representation plays a crucial role in shaping perceptions of gender."
  • "Education is key to challenging traditional gender roles."

Expressing Opinions

  • "In my view, gender equality benefits society as a whole."
  • "I believe that addressing unconscious bias is essential for progress."
  • "From my perspective, quotas can be effective but have limitations."
  • "It seems to me that cultural change is necessary alongside policy changes."
  • "I would argue that representation in media has improved but still has far to go."
  • "What strikes me is how gender expectations vary across different cultures."
  • "I'm of the opinion that both men and women face different types of gender pressures."

Discussion Topics

  • The effectiveness of quota systems in addressing gender imbalance in leadership positions
  • The role of education in challenging traditional gender roles and stereotypes
  • How media representation of gender has evolved over the past decade
  • The impact of parental leave policies on career progression for both men and women
  • Strategies for addressing unconscious bias in recruitment and promotion processes
  • The relationship between gender equality and economic development
  • Cultural differences in approaches to gender equality around the world
  • The importance of male allies in promoting gender equality in the workplace
  • The intersection of gender with other aspects of identity such as race, class, and sexuality
  • The role of technology in either reinforcing or challenging gender stereotypes
