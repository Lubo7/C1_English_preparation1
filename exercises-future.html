Future Forms Exercises

This page contains practice exercises for future forms in English. Complete these exercises to reinforce your understanding of different ways to express future time.

Exercise 1: Choose the correct future form

Select the most appropriate future form for each sentence based on the context.

  1. I ________ (meet) Sarah at the airport tomorrow. It's all arranged.
  2. Look at those clouds! It ________ (rain) soon.
  3. Don't call me between 8 and 9. I ________ (study) for my exam.
  4. By this time next year, I ________ (complete) my degree.
  5. I think it ________ (be) sunny tomorrow.
  6. The train ________ (leave) at 6:30 tomorrow morning.
  7. I ________ (help) you with your homework when I get home.
  8. This time next week we ________ (sit) on the beach in Spain.
  9. By the end of this month, she ________ (work) here for five years.
  10. I'm not sure yet, but I ________ (probably/go) to the party on Saturday.
  1. I am meeting Sarah at the airport tomorrow. It's all arranged.
  2. Look at those clouds! It is going to rain soon.
  3. Don't call me between 8 and 9. I will be studying for my exam.
  4. By this time next year, I will have completed my degree.
  5. I think it will be sunny tomorrow.
  6. The train leaves at 6:30 tomorrow morning.
  7. I will help you with your homework when I get home.
  8. This time next week we will be sitting on the beach in Spain.
  9. By the end of this month, she will have been working here for five years.
  10. I'm not sure yet, but I will probably go to the party on Saturday.

Exercise 2: Complete the sentences with the correct future form

Use the verb in brackets in the appropriate future form.

  1. By the time you arrive, I ________ (finish) the report.
  2. I can't meet you tomorrow afternoon. I ________ (attend) a meeting.
  3. I'm sure she ________ (pass) the exam. She's been studying very hard.
  4. The conference ________ (start) at 9 AM sharp tomorrow.
  5. This time tomorrow, we ________ (fly) over the Atlantic.
  6. I ________ (call) you as soon as I arrive.
  7. By 2030, scientists ________ (find) a cure for many types of cancer.
  8. Look at her! She ________ (fall) off the ladder!
  9. I ________ (not/be) available next Monday. I have a doctor's appointment.
  10. By the end of the course, we ________ (learn) over 2,000 new words.
  1. By the time you arrive, I will have finished the report.
  2. I can't meet you tomorrow afternoon. I am attending a meeting.
  3. I'm sure she will pass the exam. She's been studying very hard.
  4. The conference starts at 9 AM sharp tomorrow.
  5. This time tomorrow, we will be flying over the Atlantic.
  6. I will call you as soon as I arrive.
  7. By 2030, scientists will have found a cure for many types of cancer.
  8. Look at her! She is going to fall off the ladder!
  9. I won't be available next Monday. I have a doctor's appointment.
  10. By the end of the course, we will have learned over 2,000 new words.

Exercise 3: Identify the future form and explain its use

For each sentence, identify the future form used and explain why it's appropriate in that context.

  1. "I'm seeing the doctor at 3 PM tomorrow."
  2. "By the time we arrive, the movie will have started."
  3. "Look at those clouds! It's going to rain."
  4. "The train leaves at 8:30 tomorrow morning."
  5. "This time next week I'll be lying on a beach in Hawaii."
  1. Present continuous for future - Used for planned future arrangements that are already scheduled.
  2. Future perfect - Used for an action that will be completed before a specific time in the future.
  3. Going to future - Used for predictions based on present evidence or signs.
  4. Present simple for future - Used for scheduled events, especially timetables and programs.
  5. Future continuous - Used for an action that will be in progress at a specific time in the future.

Exercise 4: Error Correction

Identify and correct the errors in the following sentences related to future forms.

  1. I will meeting my friends tomorrow.
  2. By next year, I study English for ten years.
  3. The train will leaves at 9 PM tonight.
  4. This time tomorrow I going to sit on the beach.
  5. She will been working here for five years next month.
  6. I am going to be seeing the doctor tomorrow.
  7. When you will arrive, I will have finished.
  8. Look at the sky! It will rain soon.
  9. By the end of this week, I will finishing the project.
  10. I'm sure she pass the exam.
  1. I will meet my friends tomorrow. OR I am meeting my friends tomorrow.
  2. By next year, I will have studied English for ten years.
  3. The train leaves at 9 PM tonight.
  4. This time tomorrow I will be sitting on the beach.
  5. She will have been working here for five years next month.
  6. I am seeing the doctor tomorrow. (Simplify to avoid redundancy)
  7. When you arrive, I will have finished.
  8. Look at the sky! It is going to rain soon.
  9. By the end of this week, I will have finished the project.
  10. I'm sure she will pass the exam.
