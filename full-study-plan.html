Complete 14-Day C1 English Test Preparation Plan

This is the full detailed 14-day study plan to prepare you for your C1 English test. Each day includes morning, afternoon, and evening activities focusing on grammar, vocabulary, and practice exercises.

Day-by-Day Plan

Day 1: Assessment and Review of Tenses

Morning:

  • Complete a diagnostic test to identify strengths and weaknesses
  • Begin review of present tenses (simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous)

Afternoon:

  • Vocabulary focus: Property and Home
  • Key phrases for describing housing, property development, and location preferences
  • Practice with comparative language for discussing advantages/disadvantages of locations

Evening:

  • Grammar exercises on present tenses
  • Vocabulary flashcards for Property and Home theme
  • Listen to audio excerpt C1_1_U10_L&S_Ex2_3_4_5.mp3 and C1_1_U8_L&S_Ex2_3_4.mp3

Day 2: Past Tenses and Experiences

Morning:

  • Review of past tenses (simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous)
  • Focus on narrative tenses and sequencing events

Afternoon:

  • Vocabulary focus: Experiences and Opinions
  • Language for expressing positive and negative experiences
  • Phrases for agreeing, disagreeing, and expressing opinions

Evening:

  • Grammar exercises on past tenses
  • Writing practice: Describe a memorable experience using narrative tenses
  • Listen to audio excerpt C1_1_U10_exams_listening.mp3 and C1_1_U7_L&S_Ex2_3_4.mp3

Day 3: Future Forms and Language Learning

Morning:

  • Review of future forms (will, going to, present continuous, present simple, future perfect, future continuous)
  • Practice with predictions, plans, arrangements, and schedules

Afternoon:

  • Vocabulary focus: Motivation for Language Learning
  • Phrases for discussing language learning goals and methods
  • Vocabulary related to education and skill development

Evening:

  • Grammar exercises on future forms
  • Speaking practice: Discuss your language learning journey and future plans
  • Listen to audio excerpt C1_1_U1_Exams_Listening.mp3 and C1_1_U1_L&S_Ex3_4.mp3

Day 4: Reporting Structures and Healthcare

Morning:

  • Review of reporting structures (reported speech, reporting verbs)
  • Practice with shifting tenses, pronouns, and time expressions

Afternoon:

  • Vocabulary focus: Perspectives on the NHS
  • Healthcare terminology and phrases
  • Language for discussing systems and services

Evening:

  • Grammar exercises on reporting structures
  • Writing practice: Report on a conversation about healthcare systems
  • Listen to audio excerpt C1_1_U4_L&S_Ex2_3_4.mp3 and C1_1_U4_L&S_pronunciation_section.mp3

Day 5: Modality 1 and Social Norms

Morning:

  • Review of modal verbs for obligation, prohibition, permission (must, have to, should, ought to, can, may, might)
  • Practice with degrees of certainty and obligation

Afternoon:

  • Vocabulary focus: Social Norms and Obligations
  • Phrases for discussing rules, expectations, and social conventions
  • Language for expressing necessity and obligation

Evening:

  • Grammar exercises on modal verbs
  • Speaking practice: Discuss social norms in different cultures
  • Listen to audio excerpt C1_1_U5_L&S_pronunciation_section.mp3 and C1_1_U5_grammar_Ex6_B.mp3

Day 6: Conditionals and Workplace Scenarios

Morning:

  • Review of conditional structures (zero, first, second, third, mixed)
  • Practice with hypothetical situations and their consequences

Afternoon:

  • Vocabulary focus: Hypothetical Workplace Scenarios
  • Professional vocabulary and phrases
  • Language for discussing career choices and workplace situations

Evening:

  • Grammar exercises on conditionals
  • Writing practice: Respond to hypothetical workplace scenarios
  • Listen to audio excerpt C1_1_U6_L&S_Ex3_4.mp3

Day 7: Mid-point Assessment and Review

Morning:

  • Complete a mid-point assessment test
  • Review areas of weakness identified in the test

Afternoon:

  • Comprehensive vocabulary review of themes 1-6
  • Focus on collocations and phrasal verbs from these themes

Evening:

  • Review grammar topics covered so far
  • Create mind maps for vocabulary themes
  • Practice listening with previously challenging audio excerpts

Day 8: Emphatic Structures and Television

Morning:

  • Review of emphatic structures (inversion, cleft sentences, fronting)
  • Practice with emphasis and focus in sentences

Afternoon:

  • Vocabulary focus: Television Viewing Habits and Industry
  • Media terminology and phrases
  • Language for discussing entertainment and viewing preferences

Evening:

  • Grammar exercises on emphatic structures
  • Speaking practice: Express strong opinions about television programs
  • Listen to audio excerpt C1_1_U7_R&L_Ex9_10.mp3

Day 9: Comparing and Contrasting with Decision Making

Morning:

  • Review of structures for comparing and contrasting
  • Practice with comparative and superlative forms, parallel structures

Afternoon:

  • Vocabulary focus: Considerations When Making Choices
  • Phrases for weighing options and making decisions
  • Language for discussing advantages and disadvantages

Evening:

  • Grammar exercises on comparing and contrasting
  • Writing practice: Compare and contrast two options for a major decision
  • Listen to audio excerpt C1_1_U8_L&S_pronunciation_section.mp3

Day 10: Spelling and Pronunciation with Government Topics

Morning:

  • Review of challenging spelling patterns
  • Practice with commonly confused words
  • Focus on word stress and intonation patterns

Afternoon:

  • Vocabulary focus: Government Spending of Tax Revenue
  • Political and economic terminology
  • Language for discussing public policy and finance

Evening:

  • Pronunciation exercises with difficult words
  • Speaking practice: Discuss government spending priorities
  • Listen to audio excerpt C1_1_U8_R&L_Ex8_9_10.mp3 and C1_1_U8_vocabulary_Ex4b_boy.mp3

Day 11: Passive Structures and Gender Representation

Morning:

  • Review of passive voice structures
  • Practice with passive forms in different tenses
  • Focus on when to use passive vs. active voice

Afternoon:

  • Vocabulary focus: Gender Representation and Equality
  • Terminology related to equality and representation
  • Language for discussing social issues and change

Evening:

  • Grammar exercises on passive structures
  • Writing practice: Discuss gender representation in a field of your choice
  • Listen to audio excerpt C1_1_U9_L&S_Ex2_3_4_mod.mp3 and C1_1_U9_R&L_Ex8_FIRST_PART.mp3

Day 12: Causative Structures and Comprehensive Review

Morning:

  • Review of causative structures (have/get something done)
  • Practice with expressing causation and responsibility

Afternoon:

  • Comprehensive vocabulary review of themes 7-10
  • Focus on academic and formal vocabulary from these themes

Evening:

  • Grammar exercises on causative structures
  • Speaking practice: Use causative structures to discuss services and responsibilities
  • Review challenging listening excerpts

Day 13: Mock Test and Targeted Review

Morning:

  • Complete a full mock test under timed conditions

Afternoon:

  • Review mock test results
  • Focus on areas that need improvement
  • Targeted grammar and vocabulary review

Evening:

  • Practice with specific challenging question types
  • Review test-taking strategies
  • Final review of vocabulary flashcards

Day 14: Final Preparation

Morning:

  • Light review of most challenging grammar points
  • Quick review of key vocabulary themes

Afternoon:

  • Relaxation techniques and stress management
  • Preparation of materials needed for the test

Evening:

  • Early bedtime to ensure you're well-rested for the test
