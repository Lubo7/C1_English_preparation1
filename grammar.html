1. Review of Tenses

Present Tenses

  • Present Simple: Used for habits, general truths, and states

    "I usually study English for two hours every day."

  • Present Continuous: Used for actions happening now or temporary situations

    "I'm currently preparing for my C1 English exam."

  • Present Perfect: Used for past actions with present relevance

    "I have studied English for ten years."

  • Present Perfect Continuous: Used for ongoing actions that started in the past and continue to the present

    "I have been studying for this exam since January."

Past Tenses

  • Past Simple: Used for completed actions in the past

    "I studied English at university."

  • Past Continuous: Used for actions in progress at a specific time in the past

    "I was studying when you called."

  • Past Perfect: Used for actions completed before another past action

    "I had already studied the material before the class began."

  • Past Perfect Continuous: Used for ongoing actions that continued up to a specific time in the past

    "I had been studying for three hours when you arrived."

Common Mistakes

  • Mixing present perfect and past simple
  • Using present continuous with stative verbs
  • Incorrect formation of negative and question forms

2. Reporting Structures

Direct and Indirect Speech

  • Statements: "I am studying" → He said (that) he was studying.
  • Questions: "Are you studying?" → He asked if/whether I was studying.
  • Commands: "Study harder!" → He told me to study harder.

Reporting Verbs

Basic reporting verbs

  • say
  • tell
  • ask

Advanced reporting verbs

  • admit, deny
  • suggest, recommend
  • advise, warn
  • threaten, promise
  • agree, refuse

Tense Changes in Reported Speech

Direct Speech Reported Speech
Present → Past
Past → Past Perfect
Will → Would
Can → Could
Must → Had to

Common Mistakes

  • Forgetting to change pronouns and time expressions
  • Incorrect tense shifts
  • Using direct speech punctuation in reported speech

3. Narrative Tenses

Using Tenses in Storytelling

  • Past simple: Main events in a story

    "She walked into the room and sat down."

  • Past continuous: Background actions or setting the scene

    "It was raining heavily and the wind was howling."

  • Past perfect: Events that happened before the main narrative

    "She had prepared thoroughly before the interview."

  • Past perfect continuous: Ongoing past actions that influenced the narrative

    "She had been working there for five years when the company closed."

Time Expressions in Narratives

Suddenly Meanwhile Eventually By the time No sooner... than Hardly... when

Common Mistakes

  • Inconsistent use of tenses within a narrative
  • Overuse of the past perfect
  • Incorrect sequencing of events
