Exercises: Conditionals

Practice your understanding of conditional structures with these exercises. Test your knowledge of zero, first, second, third, and mixed conditionals, as well as alternative conditional forms.

Back to Exercises
Review Conditionals Grammar

Exercise 1: Identify the Conditional Type

Identify the type of conditional used in each sentence (zero, first, second, third, or mixed).

1. If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam.

Third conditional

2. If water reaches 100°C, it boils.

Zero conditional

3. If I won the lottery, I would travel around the world.

Second conditional

4. If you don't hurry, you'll miss the train.

First conditional

5. If I had taken that job, I would be living in Paris now.

Mixed conditional (third → second)

Exercise 2: Complete the Conditionals

Complete each conditional sentence with the correct form of the verb in brackets.

1. If I __________________ (know) the answer, I would tell you.

knew

2. If she __________________ (study) more, she would have passed the exam.

had studied

3. If it __________________ (rain) tomorrow, we'll cancel the picnic.

rains

4. If you heat ice, it __________________ (melt).

melts

5. If I __________________ (be) you, I would apologize to her.

were

6. If I had seen you, I __________________ (say) hello.

would have said

7. If she __________________ (take) that job in 2010, she would be the manager now.

had taken

8. If we __________________ (leave) now, we would get there by 5 PM.

left

Exercise 3: Sentence Transformation

Rewrite each sentence using the type of conditional indicated in brackets.

1. I don't have enough money, so I can't buy that car. (second conditional)

If I had enough money, I would buy that car.

2. She didn't study, so she failed the exam. (third conditional)

If she had studied, she wouldn't have failed the exam.

3. I will call you if I arrive early. (first conditional → third conditional)

I would have called you if I had arrived early.

4. I'm not a millionaire, so I don't live in a mansion. (mixed conditional)

If I were a millionaire, I would live in a mansion.

5. When people eat too much sugar, they gain weight. (zero conditional)

If people eat too much sugar, they gain weight.

Exercise 4: Alternative Conditional Forms

Rewrite each sentence using the alternative conditional form given in brackets.

1. If it rains, we'll stay at home. (Unless)

Unless it stops raining, we'll stay at home.

2. If you don't hurry, you'll be late. (Otherwise)

Hurry up. Otherwise, you'll be late.

3. If I were you, I would accept the offer. (In your position)

In your position, I would accept the offer.

4. If you had told me earlier, I could have helped you. (Had you...)

Had you told me earlier, I could have helped you.

5. If it weren't for your help, I wouldn't have succeeded. (But for)

But for your help, I wouldn't have succeeded.

Exercise 5: Error Correction

Each sentence below contains an error in the use of conditionals. Identify and correct the error.

1. If I would have known about the party, I would have come.

If I had known about the party, I would have come. (Remove "would" from the if-clause)

2. If I will see her, I'll tell her the news.

If I see her, I'll tell her the news. (Use present simple, not future, in the if-clause)

3. Unless you will help me, I can't finish this project.

Unless you help me, I can't finish this project. (Use present simple, not future, after "unless")

4. If I was you, I wouldn't worry about it.

If I were you, I wouldn't worry about it. (Use "were" not "was" in hypothetical conditions)

5. If she would study harder, she would pass the exam.

If she studied harder, she would pass the exam. (Use past simple, not "would" in the if-clause)

Exercise 6: Mixed Conditionals

Complete these mixed conditional sentences with the correct form of the verbs in brackets.

1. If I __________________ (not eat) so much yesterday, I __________________ (not feel) sick now.

hadn't eaten, wouldn't feel

2. If I __________________ (be) more confident, I __________________ (apply) for that job yesterday.

were, would have applied

3. If she __________________ (not break) her leg last month, she __________________ (compete) in the race tomorrow.

hadn't broken, would be competing

4. If I __________________ (know) how to swim, I __________________ (save) the child yesterday.

knew, would have saved

5. If he __________________ (not miss) the train yesterday, he __________________ (be) here now.

hadn't missed, would be
